2.0 STATEMENT OF REQUIREMENTS In order to maintain the integrity of the data from these sites it is imperative to do regular maintenance and calibration of the sensors and test them to their designed limits. It is therefore necessary for the EMF to purchase two programmable temperature chambers capable of withstanding the rigors required to operate on a continuous basis twenty four hours daily. These units must stand up to continuous 24/7/365 operation with average hourly swings in temperature from -40C to +70C, and occasionally -65C to +175C. The chamber needs to be a minimum of twenty seven cubic feet to allow for simultaneous testing of multiple sensors. The Following are minimum specifications required for the environmental chamber. Temperature Range: Control & Stability: Minimum Interior Dimensions: Voltage : Access: Interior Cabinet design: -73C to +190C +/- 0.5C Cascade refrigeration system with integral aircooled condenser, Air Flow 900 CFM 36" wide x 36" deep x 36" high 208V, single phase One 3" or 4" fully welded access port centered on each side for a total of 2 ports heliarc-welded type-304 stainless steel

Control System to include a 7" Color touch screen controller with built-in data security & safeties. Ethernet capability for remote monitor and wired, wireless, local area network or World Wide Web Ethernet connectivity to provide anytime, anywhere access using a PC or PDA device. Alarm notification system to send email and/or text phone messages in the event of a test chamber alarm. Data logging with custom file names, batch & lot numbers, operator events &

digital signatures. Automated "Ethernet" back-up of data files. Easily downloadable profiles, alarm files, audit trail files and data files to USB memory stick in a compatible .CSV file format. Ability to access data files directly from controller or PC. Product control feature to accelerate temperature cycling of the device under test. Product high/low limit protection. Selectable power failure/recovery options. Fully configurable alarm settings. Communications: EIA-232, EIA-485 or IEEE-488 GPIB compatible with National Instruments Lab VIEW software and must have drivers for Lab VIEW software included. Must have Ethernet connectivity for monitoring & control. Refrigeration: Environmentally safe refrigerants, non-flammable, and have a zero Ozone Depletion Potential (ODP). Refrigeration service taps and refrigeration pressure gauges included for easy maintenance. Must have liquid filled refrigeration gauges and refrigeration service taps for ease of service. Include chamber pressure relief/equalization vent. Heating System: must be designed to reduce thermal lag and provide rapid response to instrument demand. The heating elements are isolated from the workspace to reduce radiant influence on the test item. Electrical: All wiring between the electrical panel and the various chamber components must pass through terminal strips for easy maintenance. Wiring is to be color-coded and identified with numbers that correspond to system schematics. Multiple safety devices to include a primary over-temperature limit to protect product and an over-temperature safety limit to protect the chamber. All branch power and control circuits are to be individually fused. Safety relay connection is to be provided to protect device under test by removing power to it when the chamber is not running. Options: Must include a minimum of 2 adjustable shelves plus the standard shelf for a total of three adjustable slide-out shelves per chamber, an internal light with external switch, and a heated frost free viewing window, heavy duty casters with 4" wheels and leveling legs. 3.0 OFFERS/PROPOSALS 3.1 3.2 The Government reserves the right to reject any offer that is rated technically unacceptable for any reason.
