G. Features and Functions:

1. Shepardizing: The contractor shall provide a "shepardizing" or equivalent online service. "Shepardizing" refers to the process of determining whether a case or legal authority has been overturned, reaffirmed, questioned, or cited by later cases for validating and updating legal sources. This feature shall provide the ability to see if a case is losing its precedential value over time; the ability to view all citing references in an easy to view form on one screen; the ability to restrict findings according to issues of law, issues of fact, analysis, jurisdiction, and head note42 (or equivalent); and the ability to search full text of citing references for particular legal issues. 2. with: a. the ability to conduct a Boolean search through multiple legal sources b. the ability to download, print, e-mail, fax documents retrieved at no additional charge; c. the ability to read a summary of a case to get quick understanding of the case; and d. the ability to use a search tool that will help target a legal issue, identify an appropriate source and formulate a search request; and e. the ability to create tabs or sources or areas of law particular to the individual user Other Functions: The research service shall also provide the user

ATTACHMENT 3 EVALUATION FACTORS FOR AWARD COMPUTER ASSISTED LEGAL RESEARCH I. IN GENERAL A. Best Value: Quotes will be evaluated using the evaluation factors identified below. Award will be made to the vendor whose response represents the best value to the Government. There are three factors, Technical, Past Performance and Price. The Technical Factor is the most important factor, followed by Past Performance. The least important factor is Price. B. Equivalents: When evaluating equivalents the Government will consider the extent to which the equivalent offered is similar to the named item/service/database in meeting the government's requirement. An equivalent source shall be described with sufficient detail so that the Government can ascertain whether it is truly an equivalent. The vendor should use the footnote description associated with each source identified below, to facilitate describing an equivalent source. Failure to provide access to Mandatory Sources, or an acceptable equivalent, may result in rejection of the quote. C. Exceeding Requirements: The Agency will evaluate favorably a vendor's proposal to exceed the requirements of the SOW. One example of exceeding requirements would be if a vendor offered free admission to symposiums, conferences, etc. sponsored by the vendor. Another example would be if the vendor proposes access to sources listed in the statement of work that are not identified as "Mandatory Source." D. Demonstration: After the RFQ closes, the Government may contact an offer to schedule a demonstration of their product features to Government evaluators. The demonstration must not exceed 1 hour. All demonstrations will take place within 14 days after the close of the RFQ. To the extent the information disseminated in the demonstration conflicts with the information in the vendors written quote, the written quote shall control.
