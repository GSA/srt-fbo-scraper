2 Background The Department of Educations (ED) mission, as supported by the implementation of the No Child Left Behind Act, is to ensure equal access to education and to promote educational excellence throughout the nation. Implementation of the Migrant Student Information Exchange (MSIX) is directly relevant to EDs Strategic Plan in Goal 1, Improve Student Achievement, which is the first of three goals that support EDs mission: "The Department of Education's mission is to promote student achievement and preparation for global competitiveness by fostering educational excellence and ensuring equal access." The Office of Elementary and Secondary Education (OESE) Office of Migrant Education (OME) is responsible for administering the Migrant Education Program (MEP). The MEP is authorized under Part C of Title I of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965, as amended. ED provides grants to States to operate the MEP and provide supplemental educational services for migrant students. The statutory purposes of the MEP set forth in Section 1301 are to:  Support high-quality and comprehensive educational programs for migrant children to help reduce the educational disruption and other problems that result from repeated moves. Ensure that migrant students who move among the States are not penalized in any manner by disparities among the States in curriculum, graduation requirements, and State academic content and student academic achievement standards. Ensure that migrant children are provided with the appropriate educational services (including supportive services) that address their special needs in a coordinated and efficient manner. Ensure that migrant children receive full and appropriate opportunities to meet the same challenging State academic content and student academic achievement standards that all children are expected to meet. Design programs to help migrant children overcome educational disruption, cultural and language barriers, social isolation, various health-related problems, and other factors that inhibit the ability of such children to do well in school and to prepare such children to make a successful transition to postsecondary education or employment. Ensure that migrant children benefit from State and local systemic reforms.

QUALITY ASSURANCE SURVEILLANCE PLAN (QASP) 1 INTRODUCTION

This quality assurance surveillance plan (QASP) is pursuant to the requirements listed in the performance work statement (PWS) entitled Migrant Student Information Exchange System (MSIX). This plan sets forth the procedures and guidelines the Office of Migrant Education (OME) will use in ensuring the required performance standards or services levels are achieved by the contractor. 1.1 1.1.1 Purpose The purpose of the QASP is to describe the systematic methods used to monitor performance and to identify the required documentation and the resources to be employed. The QASP provides a means for evaluating whether the contractor is meeting the performance standards/quality levels identified in the PWS and the contractor`s Quality Control Plan (QCP), and to ensure that the government pays only for the level of services received. This QASP defines the roles and responsibilities of all members of the integrated project team (IPT), identifies the performance objectives, defines the methodologies used to monitor and evaluate the contractor`s performance, describe s quality assurance documentation requirements, and describes the analysis of quality assurance monitoring results. Performance Management Approach The PWS structures the acquisition around what service or quality level is required, as opposed to how the contractor should perform the work (i.e., results, not compliance). This QASP will define the performance management approach taken by OME to monitor and manage the contractor`s performance to ensure the expected outcomes or performance objectives communicated in the PWS are achieved. Performance management rests on developing a capability to review and analyze information generated through performance assessment. The ability to make decisions based on the analysis of performance data is the cornerstone of performance management; this analysis yields information that indicates whether expected outcomes for the project are being achieved by the contractor. Performance management represents a significant shift from the more traditional quality assurance (QA) concepts in several ways. Performance management focuses on assessing whether outcomes are being achieved and to what extent. This approach migrates away from scrutiny of compliance with the processes and practices used to achieve the outcome. A performance-based approach enables the contractor to play a large role in how the work is performed, as long as the proposed processes are within the stated constraints. The only exceptions to process reviews are those required by law (federal, state, and local) and compelling business situations, such as safety and health. A results focus provides the contractor flexibility to continuously improve and innovate over the course of the contract as long as the critical outcomes expected are being achieved and/or the desired performance levels are being met.

4.1.2

Random Sampling Random sampling evaluation is a quality assurance method designed to evaluate some, but not all, of a specific contract requirement. This method, based on statistical principles, estimates the Contractor`s overall level of performance for a given contract require ment based on a representative sample drawn from a population. Random sampling is most often used when the number of occurrences of a service is very high. 4.1.2.1 Application of Random Sampling The random sampling procedures are based on those set by the American National Standards Institute (ANSI). The random sampling procedures consider the maximum allowable deviation from the required performance standard, the level (intensity) of the evaluation effort, and the population size. There are two ways of applying random sampling for QA surveillance. The first is used only for performance evaluation and allows deductions to be taken only for observed defects; the second is random sampling for performance evaluation and deduction projection (also called extrapolated deductions), which allows deductions against the whole population based on the inspection of the sample. Problems often arise when inspectors fail to follow random sampling procedures precisely. 4.1.2.2 Performance Standards Performance standards may be specified as percentages or absolute numbers.

4.1.2.3 Evaluation Procedures Random sampling is based solely on a statistical analysis whereby a conclusion is drawn about a population based on a randomly selected sample of that population. For the conclusion to be valid, the sample selected must be representative of the population. A truly representative sample can be achieved by ensuring that the sample is selected randomly and the size of the sample is sufficient. A conclusion about Contractor performance can then be made based on the representative sample drawn. 4.1.3 100 Percent Inspection The 100 percent inspection method requires complete inspection of a contract requirement. 4.1.3.1 Application of 100 Percent Inspection These inspections should be used for requirements that are especially critical or where there is some reason for suspecting that the performance standard is not being met (and therefore, should be more closely monitored). A 100 percent inspection approach should also be used for monitoring scheduled contract requirements (such as one-time deliverables, scheduled submissions, and infrequently identified requirements). Although 100 percent inspections are the most costly, they provide positive proof of delivery of critical requirements. Evaluation schedules for 100 percent inspections will be prepared each month. 4.1.3.2 Performance Standards The performance standards may be stated as either percentages or absolute numbers. 4.1.3.3 Evaluation Procedures Observed defects for a service monitored by 100 percent inspection is compared to the performance standard. 4.1.4 Periodic Inspection Periodic inspection, or planned sampling, provides a systematic way of looking at service outputs and forming conclusions abo ut the Contractor`s level of performance in accordance with a planned schedule of surveillance. Evaluation by periodic inspection is designed to inspect some part; but not all of the products and services being monitored. 4.1.4.1 Application of Periodic Inspection Specific contract requirements that are to be monitored are selected for evaluation prior to their scheduled accomplishment. Periodic inspection differs from random sampling in the way in which samples are selected  periodic inspection sample selection is based on some subjective rationale and sample sizes are usually arbitrarily determined. With this type of evaluation, the Contractor knows that work performed in specific functions or selected locations is more likely to be monitored than work in other areas or locations, and the QAEs are able to direct efforts to those areas where inspections are most needed. Periodic inspection, as compared with random sampling, provides a less sound statistical means of making comparisons between observed and overall performance, and the Contractor`s overall lev el of performance. Periodic inspection does not accurately determine the Contractor`s overall performance. Periodic inspection is generally used in

two ways. First, it can provide a one-time subjective evaluation of Contractor performance. Second, it can be used to detect a change in the Contractor`s level of performance (e.g., trend analysis). This method requires that the sample selection criteria be well documented and consistently applied from period to period, and that there are no other intervening factors. The cost of periodic inspections varies with the level of inspections. Such latitude is important to manage limited resources and focus inspections on known or suspected problems areas. 4.1.4.2 Performance Standards Performance standards are usually stated in terms of the number of defects detected per time period, e.g., three times per month. There is no specific relationship between sample size and performance standard; however, when the performance standard is expressed as a percentage, it is recommended that the maximum sample size be chosen such that one defect does not exceed the performance standard. 4.1.4.3 Evaluation Procedures The levels of evaluation appropriate for periodic inspection are judgmental. In order to perform trend analysis from periodic inspection, criteria for sample selection should be applied consistently from period to period. To ensure valid results, the QAEs will use periodic inspection evaluation sheets and follow a detailed inspection schedule. Schedules may be developed monthly to coincide with the Contractor`s monthly schedule of work, and regularly updated after receiving the Contractor`s definitive weekly schedule. Observed defects for services monitored by periodic inspection will be totaled at the end of each month. For each service, the total number of defects will be compared to the performance standard. 4.2 Client/Customer Feedback

 Introduction & Overview

 Purpose

This Functional Requirements Document (FRD) details the requirements that will serve as the foundation for the U.S. Department of Educations (ED) Migrant Student Information Exchange (MSIX) initiative.  The functional requirements listed in this document will provide a source for communicating business objectives for MSIX, the intended behavior of MSIX, and will serve as the baseline for the design of the MSIX solution.

Background

The No Child Left Behind Act (NCLB) of 2001 mandated that ED ensure that all students have equal access to education and that ED promote educational excellence throughout the nation.  The Office of Migrant Education (OME), part of EDs Office of Elementary and Secondary Education (OESE), is responsible for administering the Migrant Education Program (MEP).  The MEP supports State Educational Agencies (SEAs) by providing funding to help SEAs establish or improve educational programs for migrant students.

Section 1308(b) of the NCLB requires the OME to assist States in developing effective methods for electronically exchanging student records amongst States; and, to determine and accurately account for the number of migratory children in each state.  OME established the MSIX initiative to satisfy these requirements and support their goals for the MEP through MSIX.

The MSIX requirements were initially developed during two user group working sessions held in  in 2004.  

Document Scope

This FRD details the baseline requirements provided to Deloitte Consulting LLP (the Deloitte Project Team) by ED for the MSIX initiative.

The Interface Control Specification (ICS) and System Requirement Specification (SRS) documents will provide details on the specific system requirements including hardware.  The date of final delivery for both ICS and SRS is January 5, 2007.  The System Security Plan will address EDs System of Records Privacy Act.



Please refer to the Requirements Management Plan for details on Deloittes approach to managing requirements.  Please refer to the Personnel and Staffing Plan for details around resource allocation tasks.  Please refer to the Contractor Project Management Plan for details around cost allocations.

Assumptions

The following assumptions have been made for the MSIX initiative.  These assumptions were previously documented in the MSIX User Requirements Document and MSIX Project Management Plan (November 3, 2006):

MSIX shall be accessible to users via the web.

System response requirements assume normal Internet connectivity and a typical response time available via the Internet; this accounts for various methods of access (e.g. dial-up, broadband and high capacity digital access (T-1 or greater)).

Data transfer will comply with the provided XML format.

State MEPs will continue to use their own systems for collecting and updating migrant student information. 

MSIX shall contain all Minimum Data Elements (MDE).  The MDEs are a proposed set of data elements that each state shall be required to collect for the purposes of the electronic transfer of migrant student information and the requirements that States shall meet for immediate access to such information.  

Secure access to MSIX shall be available to all eligible users throughout the .  

MSIX shall be developed to allow for minimum disruption and changes to existing migrant record systems.

MSIX Requirements.  The Deloitte Project Team will validate these requirements for the purposes of obtaining clarity where necessary.  Any changes required by ED to these requirements that significantly impact currently defined MSIX schedule or budget may necessitate revising the MSIX contract scope. 

Deliverables.  The Deloitte Project Team will produce the contractually documented set of deliverables.  These deliverables will be provided on or before the contractual due date. 

Timely Reviews.  Deliverable review timeline is followed and formal review processes are clearly defined.  The Department of Education and Independent Verification & Validation () staff will provide timely review comments of all draft deliverables (generally 5 business days, unless agreed otherwise).

Project Scope.  Project scope is limited to the set of requirements that have been predefined by the Department of Education.

User groups and key personnel access.  The Deloitte Project Team will have access to user groups and ED personnel.

Conflict of interest.  Contractor shall not be directed or required to perform any effort (including Unilateral Un-priced Orders) when performing the effort would entail an organizational conflict of interest or other business conflict. 

Proprietary technology and data.  The Deloitte Project Team does not anticipate the delivery of any pre-existing or proprietary technical data or computer software in connection with its performance of the contract.  If, however, and to the extent that the Deloitte Project Team delivers any of its pre-existing or proprietary technical data or computer software in connection with this opportunity, such technical data and computer software will be delivered to the Department of Education and the Government with restricted/limited rights.  Prior to such delivery of pre-existing or proprietary technical data or computer software, the Deloitte Project Team will provide notice to the Department of Education and will appropriately legend such technical data and computer software.  Moreover, in the performance of the services, the Deloitte Project Team may utilize certain pre-existing or proprietary technical data or computer software.  The Deloitte Project Team will not transfer any right, title or interest in such technical data or computer software to the Department of Education or the Government as a result of this use.  

Certification and accreditation.  ED requires certification and accreditation of the General Support System (GSS) and the Major Application (MA).  The ED Office of the Chief Information Officer (OCIO)/Information Assurance Team will handle the MSIX certification and accreditation.

Risk assessment.  The ED OCIO/Information Assurance Team will procure the independent resources to complete the MSIX risk assessment(s).

Contingencies and disaster recovery. ED will review and approve the approach for contingencies and disaster recovery.

MSIX is not the system of record for migrant student records.

Students and parents will have access to data in MSIX through authorized MSIX users.

Access to MSIX data is limited by role.

MSIX will be designed in a way that is minimally invasive to state MEP component systems.

Constraints

The following MSIX initiative constraints were previously documented in the MSIX User Requirements Document and MSIX Project Management Plan (November 3, 2006):

MSIX architecture shall link to existing systems. MSIX shall be able to link existing migrant student information systems previously developed by the states regardless of their technical architecture. 

MSIX access for non-MEP system users. MSIX shall allow registered, eligible users who do not use an existing State MEP database to query MSIX for information on migrant students who have entered their state.

Implementation schedule.  The schedule will not be impeded by unforeseen circumstances and will consist of two pilots, at six and nine months, and a nationwide rollout at 12 months after contract award.

Communication.  Maintain an open communication channel amongst all project teams. 

Certification and accreditation.  Availability of resources from the ED OCIO/Information Assurance Team to execute the MSIX certification and accreditation.  The ED OCIO/Information Assurance Team will not approve processing of "live" data in any environment until it has been accredited.

Security controls.  Security controls requiring "organization defined" parameters must be reviewed and approved by the ED OCIO/Information Assurance Team.

4.1	Goals and Objectives

ED has established the following goals and objectives for the MSIX initiative.  The requirements detailed in this document support these goals and objectives:

Goal 1:  Create an electronic exchange for the transfer of migrant student education and health data amongst the States.

Objective 1.1:  Design, develop, and build the Migrant Student Information Exchange.

Objective 1.2:  Efficiently collect and maintain complete and accurate migrant student education and health data needed for the purpose of record exchange.

Objective 1.3:  Provide users with a consolidated migrant student record in a timely manner in order to facilitate enrollment, placement, and accrual of credits for migrant students.

Objective 1.4:  Reduce the burden of collecting, maintaining, and exchanging migrant student records.

Goal 2:  Promote the use of Migrant Student Information Exchange. 

Objective 2.1:  Conduct consultation with States in the design, development, and implementation of the MSIX.

Objective 2.2:  Disseminate information on the requirements, availability, and benefits of the MSIX to prospective users.

Objective 2.3:  Provide training and help desk support to data users and providers.

Goal 3:  Ensure the use of the consolidated migrant student record for the purposes of enrollment, placement, and accrual of credit for migrant students in school and migrant education projects.

Objective 3.1:  Monitor compliance with records exchange requirements for the production of the consolidated migrant student records.    

Objective 3.2:  Monitor the use of the consolidated student record for the intended purposes of school enrollment, placement and accrual of credits. 

Objective 3.3:  Provide best practices, incentives, and penalties to continuously encourage and expand the use of the consolidated record.

Goal 4:  Produce national data on the migrant population.

Objective 4.1: Provide the stakeholders with census data and statistics on unduplicated national migrant population.

Objective 4.2:  Use unduplicated data for the generation of accurate, consistent child counts.  

4.2	Scope Summary

MSIX will support queries to a source that stores migrant student data. This data will be imported periodically from state migrant student databases.  MSIX will also provide a secure access that will enable States to exchange students MDEs for the purposes of enrollment, grade/course placement, and credit accrual.  The scope of this document does not include allocation of requirements to a specified pilot (or phase).  The pilot phase where a specified requirement is addressed will be specified in the RTM.  Requirements on hardware were not specified by ED; therefore no specifics on hardware requirements are discussed in this document.

M. 1 1. Evaluation Factors for Award

EVALUATION FACTORS

a. Evaluation of all offers will be made in accordance with the criteria outlined in this section. Proposals will be evaluated against the Government's following factors listed in descending order of importance. Technical Capability, Management Approach, Teaming/Partnering Plan, Quality Control, and Past Performance Experience when combined, are significantly more important than Price. As the technical factors become more equal amongst offerors, then price shall become a more significant factor. Factor 1: Technical Capability Factor 2: Management Approach Factor 3: Teaming/Partnering Plan Factor 4: Quality Control Factor 5: Past Performance Experience Factor 6: Price b. The Government will evaluate the offerors' capability through the utilization of both the written proposal and other written information and oral presentations. The Government will evaluate and assign a qualitative rating to the Technical Capability and Management Approach factors and sub-factors for each offeror's proposal. In addition, the Government will evaluate the risks associated with the offeror's technical proposal and assign a proposal risk rating. The Government will evaluate the offeror's past performance and assign a performance risk rating. c. Factors:

Factor 1: Technical Capability: The evaluation of the Technical Capability factor will be a composite evaluation using all of the sub-factors associated with this factor, and will assess the extent to which an offeror's proposal demonstrates a sound, practical methodology for meeting the overall requirements associated with the PWS. The Technical Capability factor and each of its sub-factors will receive an overall adjectival and proposal risk assessment rating. The Technical Capability Factor is subdivided into the following five sub-factors of equal importance: Sub-Factor 1 - Technical Understanding: The offeror will demonstrate in the written proposal and oral presentation an understanding of the work. In making the evaluation, the Government will consider an offeror's knowledge of the PWS in terms of the concept, purpose, importance, inherent problems and ideas for solutions. Sub-Factor 2 - Soundness of Approach: Proposal describes the proposed approach to comply with each of the requirements specified in the PWS. The proposal is consistent with the stated goals and objectives. The proposed approach is well documented and ensures the achievement of timely and acceptable performance in all task areas. Sub-Factor 3 - Customer Support: Proposal describes their knowledge and experience providing wellreceived and successful customer support to the migrant education community on a local, state or federal level. Knowledge of challenges in the migrant community for exchanging student records and implementing technology to support the Migrant Education Program objectives will be evaluated. Sub-Factor 4 - Equipment Replacement (hardware refresh) or Alternative: Proposal presents a well defined approach to the replacement of aging equipment during Option Period 1. Pros and cons along with associated risk are clearly addressed as they apply to equipment replacement through purchase, lease or other alternative approach (negating the need for a hardware refresh) such as the use of Cloud computing or other innovative solutions to hosting MSIX. The offeror presents a clear case for the method of choice identifying technical benefit, cost implications and risk. Sub-Factor 5 - Sample Task: The response to the sample task will be evaluated to determine the extent of the offeror's understanding of the Government's requirements and ability to perform the task. The clarity and completeness of responses in addressing the special issues and problems associated with the performance of the sample tasks will be evaluated. Sample Task:

Key personnel should be qualified according to standards specified in the PWS and effort should be adequate to successfully perform the tasks in the PWS.
