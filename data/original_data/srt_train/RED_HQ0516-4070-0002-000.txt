1. INTRODUCTORY: Defense Media Activity Marine Corps Production requires a mobile application and responsive design website to efficiently and effectively engage its six core demographics, which include active duty Marines, potential Marines, reserve Marines, retirees, family members and the general public. The app's primary focus will be to provide an engaging, interactive experience consistent with the Marine Corps' culture. The app shall facilitate habitual use and consumption of news and information, via rewards based gamification, with a primary focus on 25-year old and under Marines given this demographic which represents 61% of the Marine Corps and a primary communication objective for the Commandant. BACKGROUND: The Marine Corps Production Directorate, at the Defense Media Activity (DMA), in conjunction with Headquarters Marine Corps Division of Public Affairs, is responsible for the design, development and management of all public access digital communications platforms for the United States Marine Corps. DMA Marine Corps Production is responsible for the design, development, production, management and continuous improvement of the Corps' Public Affairs communications products. These include the Corps' flagship website (Marines.mil), flagship broadcast operations (MarinesTV), web log (Marines Blog), mobile applications and all official social media online presences. The Corps' digital products are instrumental in communicating the Marine Corps' story, building community with the American public, delivering command information to Marines, and the recruitment and retention of Marines. The products provide unique, useful and innovative information and content about operations, equipment, education and training, sports, fitness, entertainment and policy to an audience of active duty Marines, potential Marines, reserve Marines, retirees, family members and the general public. 2. GENERAL REQUIREMENTS: 2.1 The contractor shall provide all necessary services and resources for the design, development and maintenance of a new Marine Corps mobile and tablet application. The tasks under this contract include support in the areas of research and assessment, graphic design, website and application design, development/programming, deployment and maintenance. The app shall be section 508 compliant. 2.2 The period of performance is one (1) year with one (1) full option year. 2.3 The Contractor shall work in conjunction with the Government's schedule to minimize disruptions and delays. 2.4 The Contractor is not responsible for the mobile application's content. The Government will provide all content for the app either through the form of RSS feeds the app consumes and displays, web views within the app or via text files given to the Contractor for use in native code of the application.

3. SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS: 3.1 Software Requirements: Commercial (COTS) off-the-shelf software. Web-based Content Management System for the app Social sharing and feed mash-up service Sports score feed service Push notifications for all platforms Technical support (email and phone) Fully customizable COTS software Robust app analytics Estimated yearly cost of < 50K 3.2 Configuration Requirements: 3.2.1 The Contractor shall design and develop the mobile application in a way that provides a similar theme, yet device-specific experience, for users depending on what type of device they are on. The two device types supported shall be smart phones and tablets. 3.2.2 The Contractor shall develop the mobile application to run without compromise on the two latest versions, as of project kick-off, of the iOS and Android operating systems. 3.2.3 The Contractor shall understand and execute the necessary processes to develop and deploy the mobile application to Apple (iOS), Google (Android) marketplaces for the initial deliverable and then extend the offering to the Windows marketplace during one of the option year phases. 3.2.4 The app shall be a hybrid of content packaged into the app's native code as well as content that is piped in from the web using both RSS feeds and web views of externally hosted html pages. 3.2.5 The app shall work regardless of the user's connectivity to the Internet, as well as give users the option to download and save content to a personal "reading list" for access later regardless of the user's connectivity to the Internet. 3.2.6 The app shall leverage push notification capabilities to alert end-users of new and/or featured content. DMA Marines will manage these push notifications via the app CMS. 3.2.7 The Contractor shall design and develop an app home screen to highlight roughly five Marine Corps featured headlines per day that reside on the existing Marines.mil website. 3.2.8 The Contractor shall design and develop at least 8 different core sections within the app to display the following news, photos, press releases, MarinesTV Video, Messages, Orders/Directives, and social media (Facebook, Twitter, Pinterest, YouTube, Flickr, and Instagram) 3.2.9 The app shall allow users to easily share the content they consume to various social media platforms including Facebook and Twitter. Pinterest pinning should be included for photos. 3.2.10 The app shall have a capability to filter and display news and information from each of the above sections based on various meta-data (e.g. tags, categories) that exists for each content type.

3.2.11 The app shall have a keyword search capability that allows users to easily find any of the content from para 3.1 above. Search results should be sortable and filterable to allow users to narrow down and organize their search appropriately. 3.2.12 The app shall be a responsive high performance mobile application that delivers screen load times of sub 1 second. 3.2.13 The app will have a contemporary mobile application design that is professional in nature and mimics Apple's App Store design standards. 3.2.14 The contractor shall have personnel on-call to support the conduct of preliminary and final government testing of the app. Personnel will be utilized to diagnose and correct any application failures encountered during testing. Personnel should be available to provide support within 2 hours response time of a testing failure. Personnel are required to remain engaged and to assist the government until the failure is resolved. 3.2.15 The Contractor shall develop a Marines social mash-up feed, and associated front-end display, to provide end-users with the ability to consume all Marine Corps social media posts from a centralized point in the app.
