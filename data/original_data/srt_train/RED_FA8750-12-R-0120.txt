1.0 BACKGROUND 1.1 The National Air and Space Intelligence Center (NASIC) requires a full spectrum of lifecycle engineering and technical and operational support for their intelligence analysis and production mission. This PWS describes the technical and performance objectives for operations and maintenance, research and development, and intelligence production support, primarily at the NASIC, but extending to other Intelligence Community (IC) analysis and production centers utilizing NASIC-developed architectures, databases, tools and applications. 1.2 The objective of this acquisition is to provide a full spectrum of technical support to NASIC to maintain, enhance and operate the current infrastructure and applications and incorporate new capabilities required to meet future mission requirements. The ability to meet the ever-changing mission will require incorporation of new capabilities through several possible methods, such as modernization of existing capabilities, integration of external capabilities or the development of new capabilities. The development activities will require the need to research, design, prototype, develop, integrate and transition the new capabilities in order to meet an ever-increasing challenge to provide timely, accurate and actionable intelligence products and information required by warfighters in a reliable, responsive, efficient and cost effective manner. 1.3 This effort performs operations and maintenance, research and development, and intelligence production support to operate, maintain, modernize, extend the capabilities of, and utilize NASIC's current functional capabilities to include the Automated Virtual Information Production Support System (AVIPSS). AVIPSS is an integrated automated threat analysis and database production system designed to meet NASIC mission requirements. The effort to be undertaken here is a continuing part of a research and development, enhancement and sustainment program to deliver capabilities required to satisfy the demands of a continually changing production mission at NASIC in response to ever evolving threats in an equally dynamic technological environment. Operational capabilities delivered under this effort will directly support and satisfy NASIC mission responsibilities in Electronic Combat assigned by Headquarters United States Air Force 3

RIK-M001 EVALUATION CRITERIA (APR 2012) EVALUATION CRITERIA (a) Introduction. This Section outlines those factors which comprise the criteria the Government will consider in evaluating Offerors' capabilities and proposals. The criteria listed are intended to show the scope of the evaluation to be performed on proposals submitted in response to the Request for Proposal. Evaluation will be accomplished by comparing Offerors' capabilities and proposal elements against Government requirements. (b) Basis For Contract Award. This is a best value source selection conducted in accordance with tradeoff source selection processes pursuant to FAR Part 15.3, Source Selection, as supplemented by the Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (DFARS), and the Air Force Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement (AFFARS). These regulations are available electronically at the Air Force (AF) FARSite, http://farsite.hill.af.mil. The Government will select the best overall offer, based upon an integrated assessment of Technical/Technical Risk, performance confidence and Cost or Price. Contract may be awarded to the offeror who is deemed responsible in accordance with the FAR, as supplemented, whose proposal conform to the solicitation's requirements (to include all stated terms, conditions, representations, certifications, and all other information required by Section L of this solicitation) and is judged, based on the evaluation factors and subfactors to represent the best value to the Government. The Government will compare differences in the value of technical features/capabilities and performance confidence with differences in cost or price to the Government. In making this comparison the Government values superior technical features/capabilities and performance confidence greater than lowest cost or price to the Government (see FAR 15.101-1). This may result in an award to a higher rated, higher priced offeror, where the decision is consistent with the evaluation factors, and the Source Selection Authority (SSA) reasonably determines that the technical, performance confidence and/or overall business approach of the higher price offeror(s) outweighs the price difference. The SSA will base the source selection decision on an integrated assessment of proposals against all source selection criteria in the solicitation (described below). While the Government source selection evaluation team and the SSA will strive for maximum objectivity, the source selection process, by its nature, is subjective; and therefore, professional judgment is implicit throughout the entire process. In accordance with FAR 15.304(e), all evaluation factors other than cost or price, when combined, are significantly more important than cost or price. However, cost or price will still contribute substantially to the best value award decision. The Government will not make an award at a significantly higher overall cost or price to the Government to achieve only slightly superior technical features and performance confidence.
