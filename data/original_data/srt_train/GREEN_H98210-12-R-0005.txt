Performance Work Statement 1.0 INTRODUCTION

DMDC is part of a Department of Defense (DoD) Field Activity called the Defense Human Resources Activity (DHRA). DMDC's primary function is to support the information management needs of the Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Personnel & Readiness (OUSD P&R). DMDC is the DoD's enterprise human resource information source, providing secure services and solutions to support the Department's mission. 2.0 BACKGROUND

DMDC requires Video Teleconferencing (VTC) maintenance, testing, and certification for equipment in Rosslyn VA, Alexandria VA, and Seaside CA. A complete listing of these sites can be found in Exhibit A. The prior VTC maintenance support contract expired in July 2011. 3.0 SCOPE

The contractor shall provide support services for preventative, scheduled, and routine maintenance of software and hardware for existing Audio Visual and Video Teleconferencing (AV/VTC) equipment and test and certify all systems and equipment at the Government locations scheduled to relocate as part of the Base Realignment and Closure (BRAC) both pre- and post-move. The Contractor shall provide all personnel, management, and materials necessary to perform all services detailed within this PWS. 4.0 4.1 REQUIREMENTS The Contractor shall:

4.2.1 Perform two (2) preventive maintenance visits per service year to maintain the operational readiness of the system(s). The visit schedule will be developed and coordinated with the Contracting Officer's Representative (COR). During each visit, knowledge transfer of system updates/changes with government technical support staff shall be provided. Technical and hardware changes shall be reflected in the established As-built Drawings and Asset Inventory. 4.2.2 During each preventive maintenance visit, perform the following:

4.2.2.1 Perform visual inspection of all equipment and wiring/cabling; 4.2.2.2 Clean and align equipment; 4.2.2.3 Replace parts that fail to meet expectations with spare/new GFE; 4.2.2.4 Service equipment and rack cooling systems; 4.2.2.5 Recommend end-of-life cycle of equipment; 4.2.2.6 Verify connectivity of Secure/Non-Secure ISDN and IP modes; 4.2.2.7 Provide software/firmware upgrades when available; and 4.2.2.8 Provide programming updates as required to DMDC's Touch Panel and sync all sites to one standard. 4.3 Provide Corrective Maintenance/Upgrades

4.3.1 Provide system-level corrective maintenance that includes the replacement of failed components from the Government-Furnished Equipment (GFE) spare parts; audio/video system adjustments/calibrations and replacing GFE consumables such as lamps and filters, etc. The Government may request the Contractor to recommend replacement equipment when the system interface coding is impacted.

If the underlying cause is an equipment issue requiring replacement of the same, resolution will continue upon Government receipt of procured parts. 4.5.2 Non-Critical Support. If a problem cannot be resolved via the Help Desk within four (4) business hours, dispatch will occur within three business days of the determination of need for the dispatch.
