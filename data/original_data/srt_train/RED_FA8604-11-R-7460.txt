ASC/PKE-H004 GOVERNMENT DOWN TIME (JUN 2010) 1. From time to time, the Government (Installation Commander or designee) may decide to close all or part of the Government installation in response to an unforeseen emergency or other occurrence. Sample emergencies include, but are not limited to, adverse weather such as snow or flood, an act of God such as a tornado or earthquake, or a base disaster such as a natural gas leak or fire. Contractor personnel are "non-essential personnel" (except as otherwise determined in accordance with the Performance Work Statement, Section J, Attachment #1 hereof) for purposes of any instructions regarding the emergency. 2. The contractor shall be officially dismissed upon notification of closure in accordance with paragraph 4 below. The contractor shall promptly secure all government furnished property appropriately and evacuate in an expedient but safe manner. 3. With regard to work under the contract, the Government shall retain the following options: a. The Government may grant a time extension in each task order delayed by the closure equal to the time of the closure, subject to the availability of funds and period of performance limitations. b. The Government may forego the work. The contractor will not be paid for work not performed. c. The Government may reschedule the work on any day satisfactory to both parties. d. The Government may, in its discretion, permit the contractor to perform only at the contractor's facility during the period of installation closure if work within the scope of the task order can be accomplished. This work must be authorized by the prime contractor responsible for the effort. Notify the CO within 5 business days of return to work regarding the nature and scope of the work completed at the contractor's facility. If approved, the contractor shall be permitted to bill the Government at the labor rates identified in the contract. 4. Government Installation Closure Notification Procedures: a. After an official decision to close the installation has been made by the responsible Government representative, the local radio and television stations shall be notified of the closure. b. The contractor is directed to listen to or watch one of the local radio or television stations for notification of installation closures and delays. Unless otherwise notified by the CO, the Contractor should follow instructions intended for non-essential personal. c. The contractor may not receive any other form of notification of installation closure from the Government. The contractor is responsible for notification of his or her employees and arrangement for any work off-Government site as described in 3.d. above. 5. If the decision to close all or part of the installation is made during the duty day, and the installation Commanders' decision is transmitted through official notification channels, the contractor shall follow the instructions as given. Contractor personnel shall notify their task monitor and act in accordance with the task monitor's instruction. 6. Government Installation Closure Due to Non-Emergencies: The Government (Installation Commander or designee) may elect to close all or part of the installation for non-emergency reasons such as a time-off award, base open house, etc. In the event of a non-emergency installation closure, the Wing/Group/Squadron Quality Assurance Personnel (QAP) and the Contractor Task Leader shall choose a course of action within the following options: a. If there is a need for the service during installation closure and a Government employee will be

DLA TRANSACTION SERVICES

Logistics Metrics Analysis Reporting System (LMARS) And Customer Wait Time (CWT)
1.0 BACKGROUND The DLA Transaction Services designs, develops, implements and operates Automated Information Systems (AIS)s for DoD Logistics. The DLA Transaction Services Systems perform Transaction Routing, Network Interoperability, and Logistics Information services including worldwide data sharing and reporting. DLA Transaction Services performs these agile support missions for the Defense Logistics Agency (DLA), all the Military Services and Defense and Civil Agencies. During the past several years, rapid change has been introduced throughout the DoD. As a result, DLA Transaction Services has undergone significant organizational impact as part of the DoD Logistics reinvention and additional reorganizations in DLA; DLA Transaction Services parent Agency. These DoD changes have occurred over the past 6 Â­ 8 years and have resulted in an overall increase in the DLA Transaction Services workload. Additionally, new Logistics processes and practices have increased the overall complexity of the mission, as business process reengineering and other consolidation efforts have been taking place within the Services and Agencies. In addition, DoD directions to incorporate commercial practices and privatize some of the organizational functions throughout DoD have impacted the DLA Transaction Services organic end-strengths. This overall downsizing fortunately coincided with the major modernization of DLA Transaction Services Automated Data Processing Equipment (ADPE) and Automated Information Systems (AISs) allowing DLA Transaction Services to be at the cutting edge of Logistics Automation Services for its customer base. During this time of change in DoD, DLA Transaction Services is also being assigned new critical Logistics missions, functions and tasks. DLA Transaction Services is implementing these new processes and procedures transparently to its customers while maintaining previously provided support services, without interruption, at the same high standard of quality and responsiveness. 1.1 PURPOSE The Contractor will be responsible for the Logistics Metrics Analysis Reporting System (LMARS) that currently maintains logistics pipeline information for all DOD wholesale items. The Logistics Metrics Analysis Reporting System/Customer Wait Time (LMARS/CWT) System continues to need development, enhancement and support in order to provide current and additional LMARS/CWT committee reporting requirement and Top 100 (total processing time) drilldown capabilities to enable the DoD Logistics Managers to efficiently and effectively manage their processes. The current LMARS/CWT reporting system provides a set of committee-defined standard reports and drilldowns.

1

The DoD and Joint Staff are requesting more reporting and drilldown capabilities in support of strategic logistics planning goals and objectives. A suite of On Line Analytical Processing (OLAP) and Reporting module tools are being utilized to meet these demands and needs to be supported and maintained. The OLAP tool is Web Intelligence and the reporting tool is Crystal Decisions Enterprise and Reports. This SOW will provide for the professional support to continue to integrate, enhance, maintain and support these tools and to provide sustainment of the existing LMARS/LRT/CWT reporting capability and establish the structure for future reporting requirements. All work performed under this contract will be done in accordance with the baselines established in the DLA Transaction Services. Any changes to these baselines may impact performance.

1.0 Basis for Contract Award. This acquisition will utilize the Lowest Price, Technically Acceptable (LPTA) source selection procedure, in accordance with FAR 15.101-2, Lowest Price Technically Acceptable Source Selection Process. The offerer's technical and cost proposals will be evaluated to make an award decision. This is a competitive source selection to award a task order under a GSA Federal Supply Schedule (FSS) in which competing Offerors' past performance history, technical competence and ability to deliver appropriately security-cleared personnel has been thoroughly evaluated. Cost proposals will be evaluated for reasonableness. The proposal with the lowest cost that has been determined to be reasonable will be evaluated for technical merit. Technical proposals will be reviewed to determine whether the respondents understand the requirements and are capable of performing the requirements. Award will be made to the Offeror who is deemed responsible in accordance with the Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) Part 9, as supplemented, whose proposal conforms to the solicitation's requirements (to include all stated terms, conditions, representations, certifications, and all other information required by Section L of this solicitation), is judged technically acceptable and whose bid represents the lowest cost to the Government. While the Government source selection evaluation team and the Source Selection Authority (SSA) will strive for maximum objectivity, the source selection process, by its nature, is subjective and, therefore, professional judgment is implicit throughout the entire process. 1.1 It is the Government's intent to award without discussions. However, the Government reserves the right to conduct discussions if later determined by the Contracting Officer to be necessary. 1.2 The Government intends to award a single contract to perform all of the functions defined in this solicitation; however, the Government reserves the right to award no contract at all, depending on the quality of the proposals submitted or budgetary limitations. 1.3 The Government may reject any proposal that is evaluated to be unrealistic in terms of program commitments, including contract terms and conditions, or unrealistically high or low cost when compared to Government estimates, such that the proposal is deemed to reflect an inherent lack of competence or failure to comprehend the complexity and risks of the program. 1.4 The Government will consider, throughout the evaluation, the correction potential of any proposal rated as "Reasonably Susceptible of Being Made Acceptable", or "Unacceptable" as those ratings are defined in paragraph 3.3 below.
