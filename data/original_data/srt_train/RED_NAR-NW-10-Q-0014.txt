BACKGROUND: The Special Media Archives Services Division (NWCS) of Access Programs (NWC) of the National Archives and Records Administration
(NARA) is replacing several old and worn flatbed film viewers used for reviewing and appraising new accessions, as well as quality checking
archival sets of films against their Reference and Intermediate videos.  This may include using an appropriate CCD camera to capture the image
directly from the prism and record out to VHS or DVD.

                                                      ATTACHMENT 1 – SALIENT CHARACTERISTICS


                                                      Motion Picture Flatbed Viewing Tables

1.0 INTRODUCTION
The Special Media Archives Services Division (NWCS) of Access Programs (NWC) of the National Archives and Records Administration (NARA) is
replacing several old and worn flatbed film viewers used for reviewing and appraising new accessions, as well as quality checking archival sets
of films against their Reference and Intermediate videos.  This may include using an appropriate CCD camera to capture the image directly from
the prism and record out to VHS or DVD.

3.0 SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

3.1 (16mm) 6 Plate Archival Flatbed Viewing Table
    • Must have six (6) 2,000’ film plates

    • Must have six (6) US Style Bobbin Core (Clutch)

    • Must have Framing Knob

    • Must have 24-sided hollow prism

    • Must have Formats: 16 normal and Super 16

    • Must have Transport sound speeds:  16, 18, 20, 22, 24, & 30fps

    • Must have Transport variable speed: 3-100 fps

    • Must have Comopt/Commag/ separate transport for Optical track (front transport) and separate transport for Magnetic track (middle
      transport)

    • Must have Shrinkage Measurement Capability w/ Shrinkage Database & Computer Cable

    • Must have Digital Counter

    • Must have PTR Rollers

    • Must have Amplifier and Speaker

    • Exciter Lamp: Red LED Diode (Cyan & Regular)
