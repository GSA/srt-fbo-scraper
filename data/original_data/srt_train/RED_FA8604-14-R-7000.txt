INTRODUCTION/BACKGROUND

Defense Logistics Agency (DLA) Transaction Services designs, develops, and implements logistics solutions that improve customers' requisition processing and logistics management processes worldwide. In the continuing effort to provide and expand these services to our customers, DLA Transaction Services has been utilizing IBM Websphere Transformation Extender (WTX) Electronic Commerce (EC) translator for the conversion of Defense Logistics Management System (DLMS) standard X12 transactions to MILS documents and the reverse. With this capability, DLA Transaction Services acts as a Gateway for the DoD Services/Agencies and civil organizations and performs translations of electronic documents to/from formats specified by each of these trading partners. DLA Transaction Services also maps and translates ANSI ASC X12 transactions to/from a number of User Defined Formats (UDFs), various versions of XML, as well as MILS and service unique data. DLA (Defense Logistics Agency) primarily uses the COTS IBM Websphere Transformation Extender (WTX) EC package to perform the functions required to conduct electronic commerce (EC) between DLA's Inventory Control Points (ICPs), and its commercial and government trading partners. This includes the Defense Supply Center Columbus (DSCC), Defense Supply Center Philadelphia (DSCP), Defense Supply Center Richmond (DSCR), and the Defense Energy Support Center (DESC). Each of these activities has had their separate WTX systems, including map processing and trading partner information, migrated to DLA Transaction Services for centralized operation and maintenance. DLA Transaction Services translates DLA application specific User Defined Formats (UDFs) to/from ANSI ACS X12 formats for these sites, and then uses the DoD Global Exchange (GEX) software for routing the X12 transactions to the trading partner community. Therefore, DLA Transaction Services must also maintain the DLA ICP trading partner information. 2.0 SCOPE DLA Transaction Service's requirement is to continue the identification, development, unit/system testing, troubleshooting, and maintenance of the Electronic Commerce Infrastructure (ECI). The Contractor shall provide daily support and maintenance of the formats and business rules required for customer's data transformations. The mapping and input/output file layouts used within the ECI must be documented. Each file layout map and accompanying business rules must be built using WTX or the Government's currently used translation toolset. The Contractor shall provide life-cycle support for the conversion of the existing Defense Logistics Management System (DLMS) standard X12 logistics ICs to XML formats and verification that all business rules are fully implemented.
