Background



The FBI has a requirement for an Internet-based news monitoring service, which shall collect and furnish real-time television and internet broadcast information from the sources reflected below.   The storage/archival system must have the capability of storing up to six (6) months of broadcast/internet media.  This is to cover international, national, and local television markets.   This service shall allow FBI employees the ability to search, retrieve, view, download, disseminate, and archive broadcast material.

Provide an Internet-based television and internet broadcast monitoring service, which shall store and operated on the contractor's mainframe. No material, software, or hardware is to be housed on FBI networks or in FBI facilities;

Provide users with the ability to download an unlimited number of media clips directly to their computer’s hard drive

39.201  	Scope of subpart. 

(a) This subpart implements Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 (), and the Architectural and Transportation Barriers Compliance Board Electronic and Information Technology (EIT) Accessibility Standards (36  Part 1194). 

(b) Further information on Section 508 is available via the Internet at . 

(c) When acquiring EIT, agencies must ensure that— 

(1) Federal employees with disabilities have access to and use of information and data that is comparable to the access and use by Federal employees who are not individuals with disabilities; and 

(2) Members of the public with disabilities seeking information or services from an agency have access to and use of information and data that is comparable to the access to and use of information and data by members of the public who are not individuals with disabilities.
